VÉLIZY-VILLACOUBLAY OVERVIEW
===============================

LOCATION: Yvelines department, Île-de-France region, France
POPULATION: 23,011 (2023)
AREA: 893 hectares
GENTILÉ: Véliziens

KEY FACTS:
- Major economic hub with Inovel Parc (1,000 companies, 45,000 employees)
- Important aerospace history (Base aérienne 107 Villacoublay)
- Well-connected transportation: A86, RN118, RER C, Tramway T6
- Significant green spaces: 313 hectares of forest + 65+ hectares parks
- Westfield Vélizy 2 shopping center (opened 1972)

HISTORICAL MILESTONES:
1793: 168 inhabitants
1921: 1,487 inhabitants
1937: Fusion of Vélizy and Villacoublay
1962: Construction of first large housing developments
1972: Opening of Vélizy 2 shopping center
1991: Creation of IUT de Vélizy
2023: Current population of 23,011

MAJOR COMPANIES:
- Thales (Aerospace)
- Safran Landing Systems
- PSA Peugeot Citroën (Technical Center)
- Dassault Systèmes
- Oracle
- BMW/Mini France
- Carmat (Medical technology)

EDUCATIONAL INSTITUTIONS:
- Institut universitaire de technologie (IUT) de Vélizy
- Institut des sciences et techniques des Yvelines (ISTY)

TRANSPORT CONNECTIONS:
- Roads: A86, RN 118, RN 12
- Rail: RER C at Chaville - Vélizy station
- Tram: T6 with 7 stations in commune
- Buses: RATP, Paris-Saclay, Vélizy Vallées networks

URBAN AREAS:
Six residential districts:
1. Mozart
2. Le Clos  
3. Le Mail (with Pointe Ouest)
4. Le Village
5. Est (with Louvois and Europe)
6. Vélizy-Bas (with l'Ursine and le Bocage)

This commune has transformed from a rural area of 168 people in 1793 to a major suburban economic center of over 23,000 people today, with significant industrial, commercial, and residential development.
